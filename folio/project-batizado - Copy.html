The 2013 2nd International Capoeira Festival will hosted by the Brazilian Arts Center in Charlotte, NC.

This year, the Brazilian Arts Center is proud to announce that its annual event will be featuring two very important guests in capoeira:

Mestre Accordeon

Recognized as one of the first capoeira masters to come to the United States, his work includes over 9 CDs as well as several books. He is currently working on a documentary about his bicycle trip from Berkeley, CA to Bahia, Brazil at the age of 70.

Mestre Suassuna

The founder of one of the largest capoeira groups in the world, Cordão de Ouro, he is recognized as the first master to record a capoeira album, and has had a very influential impact on capoeira over the past 20 years.


The event is being held August 8th – 10th at the University of North Carolina Charlotte’s campus. The schedule is listed on the Facebook event page and at www.charlottecapoeira.com. This exciting event showcases the talent, hard-work, and dedication of the Brazilian Art Center students and brings together many capoeira masters, teachers, and students from all over the world.

In addition to hosting these two Mestres, there will be workshops on capoeira, Brazilian culture, Brazilian history, and the Batizado ceremony. A Batizado is a capoeira ritual of “baptizing” a new student into capoeira by receiving their first cord, which represents their starting point in their learning experience in the world of capoeira. This ceremony allows older and more experienced students the opportunity to climb the ranks according to how much effort they have invested in their capoeira journey.
